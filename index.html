Wasaga Beach, Ontario

  • Wasaga and the sorrounding area is one of the Greater GTA's fastest growing communities. Therefore Wasaga is a proven stable and growing market for residential and commercial development.

    According to the 2011 Census, the municipal population of Wasaga Beach was approximately 17,537, the majority of whom live in the town proper. The population has increased by ±16.7% since the 2006 Census, which is well above the overall provincial increase of 5.7% during the same time period. Similar population growth is expected in the short run as retirees continue to relocate to the Wasaga Beach area. According to provincial estimates, the population of Wasaga Beach is projected to reach 27,500 by 2031.

  • Wasaga Demographics

click to enlarge

The town of Wasaga Beach is a vibrant town in Simcoe County, Ontario located on the southerly shore of Georgian Bay about 140 km’s north or downtown Toronto.Wasaga is approximately two hours north of Toronto at a modest speed. Neighbouring communities include; the town of Collingwood to the west, Stayner to the south, Orillia to the east and Barrie to the south east.

Wasaga Beach

The longest freshwater beach in the world, For years Wasaga Beach has been a prominent summer vacation destination close to the GTA markets, attracting close to 2 million visitors annually

Located at the bottom end of Georgian Bay one of the most pristine and accessible bodies of fresh water in the Great Lakes system

This 14 km of beautiful sand beach, associated dune system and extensive adjacent parklands has become somewhat of an icon for Ontarians for summer fun

Blue Mountain

Collingwood/Blue Mountains Area (30Km to the West of Wasaga Beach, attracts approximately 1.4 million visitors split between winter & summer.

Within a 35 minute drive of Wasaga Beach Blue Mountain is Ontario's largest mountain resort. With 36 runs, 3 half-pipes, a snow-tubing park and 15 ski lifts it attracts a significant amount of visitors each year.

Camping

The Wasaga area has 12 campgrounds with over 1446 fully serviced sites.

In addition it is home to the first provincial park in Canada to receive Blue Flag status for environmental standards.

Snowmobile

With a long history of snowmobiling in the area. The Wasaga area has over 106km of total trails. Every January the Town of Wasaga Beach hosts Sledfest, 2 full days of racing with over 6000 fans and 400 racers.
